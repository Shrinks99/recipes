### Ingredients

#### The Turkey

- 1 fresh roasting turkey, giblets included (12–16lbs)
- Upholstery needle or turkey sewing needle
- cotton thread

#### The Stuffing

This is for a 11 lb bird.

- 8 cups of breadcrumbs (stale cubed bread)
- 1 cup chopped celery
- 1 chopped onion
- 1/2 cup finely chopped flat parsley
- 1/2 an apple chopped
- 1 tsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. pepper
- 1 tsp. thyme
- 1 tsp. marjoram
- 1 tsp. savory
- 1/2 cup butter or margarine melted
- 2 tblsp. extra butter for rubbing the outside of the bird
- 1/4 cup water (add least slowly while mixing)

These quantities are scaled up 2.5x for a 14-16lb bird.

- 20 cups of breadcrumbs (stale cubed bread) = 1 loaf white (or sourdough) and 1 loaf brown (prep the night before and leave out to dry out)
- 2 1/2 cup chopped celery
- 2 large chopped onions
- 1 1/2 cup finely chopped flat parsley
- 1 good sized apple chopped (honey crisp or golden delicious was good)
- 1 1/2 tsp. salt
- 1-1 1/2 tsp. pepper
- 2 1/2 tsp. thyme
- 2 1/2 tsp. marjoram
- 2 1/2 tsp. savory
- 2 1/2 cup butter or margarine melted
- 2 tblsp. extra butter for rubbing the outside of the bird
- 1/2 cup water (add least slowly while mixing)

#### The Gravy Part 1

- Giblets and neck from the interior of the bird, discard the liver.
- One onion quartered
- Two large carrots, washed, cut into rounds
- Two stalks of celery, cut into small stalks
- Bouquet garni (fresh herbs tied together eg. parsley or thyme)
- Good pinch of salt
- Cover with water

### Instructions for Gravy Part 1

1. Bring pot to boil
2. Turn down heat and simmer for several hours, lid on loosely
3. Check water levels periodically
4. Strain stock into a container and reserve for later.

### Instructions for Turkey and Stuffing

Preheat oven to 425°F with bottom rack removed, when the bird is about to be stuffed.

1. Brine the turkey in cold heavily salted water for at least 1 hour. Clean sink and fill with cold water and add 1/4 cup of salt. Add turkey to the water.
2. While the turkey is in the brine, mix the stuffing ingredients.
3. Remove from water at pat dry inside and out with paper towels. Outside must be dry for the butter rub to adhere t the skin.
4. Start with the main cavity and stuff stuffing into the bird, fairly firmly, but not too tightly.
5. With needle and string, sew main cavity closed.
6. Flip bird over and put remaining stuffing into neck cavity and sew closed.
